This new result is to be contrasted with those published by Sekihara et al (2005) and
Greenblatt et al (2005). They showed that under pure measurement noise, sLORETA is
biased, and only attains exact localization under ideal conditions of no noise. They did not
consider the more realistic case where the brain in general is always active, as modeled here
by the biological noise. Under these arguably much more realistic conditions, sLORETA is
unbiased.
